Our Story

Why Sree Vidyadhiraja Trust Exists

In this era when many of our young people have migrated to other places and countries to elevate their lives, they are unable to take their aged parents with them. At the same time, the parents are unwilling to leave their native place and are emotionally unable to part from it.

Taking into consideration the needs and wishes of these concerned young people, respected Mr. Amsi Madhu, Mr. Vijayakumar, Mr. Rajendran Nair, and Ms. Sakundalabai felt that a dedicated old-age home should be established for our community to care for such parents.

As a result, in the year 2022, the Sree Vidyadhiraja Trust was formed and registered at the Verkilambi Sub-Registrar Office under Registration No. 6/2022.

Our Generous Donors

Land Donors

Two compassionate sisters who made this mission possible

Founder & Land Donor

Ms. Sakundalabai

Half an acre of land was generously donated by Ms. Sakundalabai and her sister Mrs. Sathyabhama from their family property at Pulluvilai, Perinchakonam to establish this old-age home for the Malayali community in Kanyakumari District.

"May this land become a sanctuary of peace and dignity for those who have no one."
Land Donor

Mrs. Sathyabhama

Following her sister's noble example, Mrs. Sathyabhama joined in this sacred mission by contributing her share of family land. Her generosity reflects the true spirit of "Jeevakaarunyam Jeevithathiloode" — Charity through Life.

"True wealth is not what we keep, but what we give for the welfare of others."

Sree Vidyadhiraja Trust stands on half an acre of land donated by these two sisters. A building of approximately 850 square feet and a prayer hall of about 2,000 square feet have been constructed. The Chattampi Swami Temple and Sree Krishna Swami Temple are in their final stages.
